🎓 Finite Element Analysis (FEA) of an Aircraft Wing
🔍 Overview
This project presents a structural and dynamic analysis of an aircraft wing using the Finite Element Method (FEM). The study evaluates wing performance under Wing-Up and Wing-Down loading conditions, along with mesh, element, and material sensitivity analyses to optimize structural integrity, accuracy, and efficiency.
________________________________________
🧠 Objectives
•	Perform structural analysis to evaluate stress and displacement distribution 
•	Conduct mesh convergence studies for solution accuracy 
•	Compare linear (4-node) and quadratic (8-node) finite elements 
•	Investigate material performance using aerospace alloys 
•	Carry out modal analysis to determine natural frequencies and mode shapes 
________________________________________
⚙️ Tools & Setup
•	Software: ANSYS Workbench 2020 R1 
•	Loading Conditions: 
o	Wing-Up: Lift-induced upward loading 
o	Wing-Down: Downward loading (weight/ground effects) 
•	Yield Strength Reference: 345 MPa 
________________________________________
🧪 Methodology
•	Mesh Sensitivity Study:
Evaluated element counts (211, 549, 8,617) to assess convergence of stress and displacement results 
•	Element Type Comparison:
Compared linear (4-node) and quadratic (8-node) elements for accuracy and computational efficiency 
•	Material Study:
Tested aerospace materials: 
o	Aluminum 2024-T3 
o	Aluminum 7075-T6 
o	Titanium Ti-6Al-4V 
•	Modal Analysis:
Extracted natural frequencies and mode shapes for dynamic behavior assessment 
________________________________________
🖥️ Key Results
•	Maximum stress reached approximately 1.11 GPa, exceeding the yield limit (345 MPa), indicating the need for design/material optimization 
•	Mesh refinement improved stress localization and solution accuracy significantly 
•	7075-T6 showed superior stress resistance in wing-up loading conditions 
•	Ti-6Al-4V exhibited the lowest deformation (0.241 mm), indicating high stiffness 
•	2024-T3 performed well under wing-down conditions with cost efficiency advantage 
•	First natural frequency observed at approximately 0.287 Hz, with higher modes increasing progressively 
________________________________________
📈 Key Insights
•	Material selection strongly influences structural safety and stiffness trade-offs 
•	Mesh refinement is essential for capturing realistic stress distributions 
•	Modal analysis is critical to avoid resonance and ensure structural stability 
•	High-fidelity FEM modeling improves reliability of aerospace component design
